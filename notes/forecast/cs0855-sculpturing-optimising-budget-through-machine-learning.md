# Sculpturing: Optimising Budget through Machine Learning
**Foodpanda** · 2024 · [source](https://medium.com/foodpanda-data/sculpturing-optimising-budget-through-data-analysis-bd1c1872a36b)

## Problem
Foodpanda's Marketplace Balance Project aims to optimize marketing/incentive budget by balancing demand and supply in the food-delivery marketplace. This article ("Sculpturing") is the project-management companion to the technical "The Making" piece (cs0864).

## Approach / System design
The article focuses on how the project was set up and run rather than model architecture: identifying stakeholders, defining objectives, building a project charter (background, scope, deliverables), and running delivery with Agile/Kanban to stay adaptable to unclear, shifting requirements.

## Key decisions
- Scope management: deliberately exclude volatile, uncontrollable variables (weather, time-limited offers, competitor campaigns) and focus on controllable factors.
- Methodology: Agile to accommodate evolving requirements.
- Communication: transparency via Slack, presentations, and regular stakeholder feedback loops.

## Stack
Not covered in the source (this article is about process, not tooling).

## Results
No quantitative results or model metrics are reported; the piece emphasizes successful delivery and stakeholder alignment. The technical results live in the companion article (cs0864).

## Takeaways
A budget-optimization ML effort is as much scoping and stakeholder management as modeling: cutting uncontrollable variables out of scope and running an Agile feedback loop is what kept the project tractable. Read alongside "The Making" (cs0864) for the modeling side.
